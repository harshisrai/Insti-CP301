# IIT Ropar College Structure

This document is derived strictly from the institute's official mentor program data (`ismp_content.json`). It outlines the organizational structure, clubs, fests, and campus facilities.

## 1. Governance & Oversight
### Students' Gymkhana
The umbrella organization for all student activities, functioning through the Student Legislative Council (SLC) and the Executive Council.

### Student Legislative Council (SLC)
The apex representative and policy-making body of the student community.
- **Composition**:
    - **Executive Members**: President, 7 General Secretaries (BOCA, BOSA, BOST, BOLA, BOHA, BOAA), and Research Secretary.
    - **Advisory Council**: Representatives from PhD (4th yr+, upto 3rd yr, Married), B.Tech (1st to 4th yr), M.Tech (1st & 2nd yr), and M.Sc./MS (1st & 2nd yr).
- **Key Function**: Apex policy formation and oversight of all student committees/clubs. Led by a Convenor (elected from SLC, non-executive).

### Executive Council
The executive wing of the Student Council, responsible for day-to-day coordination and leadership.
- **President**: Leads the Student Council, SLC, and Executive Council. Representative in administrative and disciplinary bodies.
- **7 General Secretaries**: Each heading a dedicated Board (BOCA, BOSA, BOHA, BOST, BOLA, BOAA, and Research Secretary).

### Advisory Board (Student Affairs)
- **Members**: Dean/Associate Dean of Student Affairs (Chair), Chief Warden/Faculty Advisors of all 6 Boards, and Registrar/Assistant Registrar.
- **Special Invitees**: Sports Officer, Security Officer.

### Institute Student Mentorship Program (ISMP)
A student initiative providing guidance to freshers on academics and campus life. Every fresher is assigned a senior mentor.
- **Induction Activities**: Mentor interactions, club orientations, sports sessions, field trips, treasure hunts, and cultural nights.
- **Mentoring Code**: Focus on trust, confidentiality, and professional/ethical conduct.

## 2. Clubs & Organizations

    - **Club Secretary**: Max 1 per club.
    - **Representative**: Max 1 per club.
    - **Mentors**: Multiple positions allowed.
    - **Coordinators**: Multiple positions allowed.

- **Automotive Club**: Vehicle design and engineering.
- **CIM**: Computer Integrated Manufacturing.
- **Coding Club**: Programming and development.
- **Esportz Club**: Organized gaming.
- **FinCOM**: Finance and commerce.
- **Iota Cluster**: Technical innovation group.

- **Robotics Club**: Automation and robot building.
- **Softcom**: Software and communication.

### Board of Academic Affairs (BOAA)
- **Leadership**: Led by the **General Secretary, BOAA**.
- **Structure**: Consists of **Branch Representatives** for every batch (Year-wise).
- **Representation Model**:
    - **Batches Covered**: All undergraduate years (e.g., 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025).
    - **Branch Representatives**: Each branch (Civil, Chemical, CSE, Electrical, Mechanical, etc.) has one or more representatives per year.
- **Specific Branches Represented**:
    - AI / AI & Data Engineering

    - Chemical Engineering
    - Computer Science & Engineering

    - Mathematics and Computing

    - Metallurgical & Materials Engineering (MME)

### Research Secretary (PhD representative)
- **Role**: Represents PhD scholars' issues across all departments.
- **Functions**: Member of SLC, ACRPGS, and placement coordination for PhDs. Handles issues related to CRF (Central Research Facility) and DRF.

## 6. Governance Processes
### Elections
- **Timeline**: Annual (at the beginning of the academic year).
- **Stages**: 
    1. **Screening**: Verification of candidature eligibility by the Election Commission.
    2. **General Elections**: Direct voting by the General Body (full-time students).
- **Eligibility**: Min CGPA of 6.5, no academic arrears/backlogs, no disciplinary record.
- **Code of Conduct**: Strict rules against communal/political campaigning, bribery, or defacing property.

### Decision Making
- **Principle**: Consensus is primary. Voting is used if consensus fails.
- **Quorum**: Requires presence of Chairman/Dean SA and at least 50% of the body's strength.
- **Tie-breaker**: Decision of the Chairman/Dean SA is binding.

### Impeachment
- **Grounds**: Capability concerns, disciplinary actions, or objectionable behavior.
- **Process**: Can be initiated by Dean SA or by a 2/3rd majority recommendation from the SLC.
